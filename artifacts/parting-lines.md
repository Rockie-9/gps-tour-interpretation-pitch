# Parting lines — three intensities × six tiers

The line a staff member says as the visitor leaves (scenario D2 — Departure). Three intensities per tier; the tier of the staff member determines the maximum intensity available, and the read of the visitor determines which intensity to use.

Register tuned per `/spec/i18n.md` §8 (visitor-facing JP uses 尊敬語 + 丁寧語).

---

## Why three intensities

Visitor reads tell you whether they want:
- **Brief** — they're in a hurry, in a group, or polite-distant. Short close.
- **Standard** — average visit; standard farewell.
- **Extended** — they engaged deeply, asked questions, lingered. Earn a longer parting that gives the experience shape.

Using the wrong intensity is worse than using a lower one. A clerk should never deliver an L5 parting — it would feel performative without the encounter to justify it.

---

## L0 — Clerk

Maximum intensity: **brief**. L0 is operationally correct, not poetic.

| Intensity | TC | EN | JP |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brief | 謝謝。 | Thank you. | ありがとうございました。 |

That's it. No standard, no extended. An L0 should not attempt them.

## L4 — Experience Designer

Maximum intensity: **extended with shape**.

L4 adds: a pre-arranged object, a pre-arranged sight-line, or a pre-arranged piece of timing referenced in the parting. The parting closes the choreography.

| Intensity | TC | EN | JP |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brief | 慢走，今天的安排，希望您喜歡。 | Take care. I hope the arrangement worked for you today. | お気をつけて。本日の段取り、ご満足いただけましたら幸いでございます。 |
| Standard | 慢走，今天的座位、節奏、那三件展品 — 都是為您今天這個下午設計的。 | Take care. Today's seat, the pace, those three pieces — all designed for your afternoon. | お気をつけて。本日のお席、間合い、そして三つの作品 — すべて、お客様の本日の午後のために設えられたものでございました。 |
| Extended | 慢走，今天讓您看到的這一段，是我們館十年最珍視的一條線索；下次您回來，我會記得這個下午。 | Take care. What we showed you today is the thread our museum has cared for most over ten years. Next time you return, I will remember this afternoon. | お気をつけて。本日ご覧いただいた一節は、当館が十年にわたり最も大切に守ってまいりました糸口でございます。次にお越しいただく際、私はこの午後を覚えております。 |

## L5 — Cultural Ambassador

Maximum intensity: **extended with continuation**.

L5 may extend the parting with: a promised follow-up (post-visit signal — see `./post-visit-signal.md`), or a named introduction to a colleague at another region, or an open invitation to a future event.

| Intensity | TC | EN | JP |
|---|---|---|---|
| Brief | 慢走。 (deliberately understated — the L5 ambassador knows when to let restraint speak.) | Take care. | お気をつけて。 |
| Standard | 慢走，明天的這個時候，我會在您住的飯店留下一張卡片；上面有我今天最想跟您說但時間沒夠的那一句。 | Take care. Tomorrow at this time, a card will be at your hotel — with the line I most wanted to say today but didn't have time for. | お気をつけて。明日のこの時刻、ご宿泊先に一枚のカードがお手元に届きます — 本日、最もお伝えしたかった一文をしたためてございます。 |
| Extended | 慢走。今天我們從這件作品開始；下一次您來，我們從這件作品結束之後的下一件開始。如果您去到京都，請去找田中愛子，她是這個系統裡跟我們有同樣語言的人。 | Take care. We began with this piece today; next time you visit, we begin with the piece that follows it. ## Operational rules

1. **Never deliver above your tier.** An L1 explainer reciting an L4 parting feels performative and breaks trust.
2. **Match the visitor's energy.** A brief visitor gets a brief parting even from an L5.
3. **The L5 "brief" is deliberately the same as L0's.** The mastery is knowing when to use it.
4. **L4 extended and L5 extended reference real things.** Never promise a card unless `POST_VISIT_SIGNAL` will actually be sent.

## Validation

The parting is observed (via `OBSERVE_BARS` event, capability 12 Experience Closure). Anchor levels L3+ require the parting to land at appropriate intensity for the visitor read.
