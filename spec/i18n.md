## 3. Measurements

| Domain | Use |
|---|---|
| Distance | metres in TC/JP body; metres + feet (in parens) in EN body |
| Volume | litres in TC/JP; litres + US gallons (in parens) in EN |
| Temperature | °C in TC/JP body; °C + °F in EN body |
| Time | 24-hour in all; visitor-facing EN may use 12-hour |
| Weight | kg in all; lb in EN if visitor-facing |

Rule: when a TC source uses a metric unit, EN renders metric + imperial in parens (no conversion in JP). When TC/JP use a culturally-specific unit (e.g. 坪 for floor area), EN uses square metres + (sq ft); JP keeps 坪.

## 4. Date formats

| Audience | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Internal logs (all langs) | ISO 8601 with timezone | `2026-05-17T14:32:18+08:00` |
| TC body | YYYY年MM月DD日 | `2026年5月17日` |
| EN body | DD Month YYYY | `17 May 2026` |
| JP body | YYYY年MM月DD日 | `2026年5月17日` |
| Roadmap chips | Quarter notation | `Q2 · 2026` (any lang) |

Year-only references: use `2026` (no localisation needed). When discussing a fiscal year: `FY2026` (English label acceptable in all langs; or 2026年度 in JP).

## 6. Name order

| Language | Order | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| TC | Family-given (no space): `林怡萱` | No comma, no spacing |
| EN | Given-family (with space): `Lin Yi-Hsuan` | OR `Yi-Hsuan Lin` — when the person is Asian, prefer family-given even in EN if known preference; otherwise given-family |
| JP | Family-given (with space): `田中 愛子` | Romanised in EN as `Aiko Tanaka` (given-family) per Japanese cultural convention for non-Japanese audiences |

Recognition tier examples (in Slide 16 and visitor ladder slide 17) follow this rule.

## 7. Address format

| Region | Order | Example |
|---|---|---|
| TW | National → Postcode → City → District → Road → No. → Floor → Person | `Taiwan 100, Taipei City, Zhongzheng District, Beiping E. Road, No. 123, 5F, Mr. Lin` |
| JP | National → Prefecture → City → District → Block-Lot-Room → Person | `日本 〒100-0001 東京都千代田区千代田1-1-1 田中様` |
| DE | National → Postal-code City → Street + No. → Person | `Deutschland, 10117 Berlin, Unter den Linden 1, Herr Müller` |
| CN | National → Province → City → District → Road → No. → Person | `中國 北京市朝陽區建國路 1 號 王先生` |

V2 ships TW/JP. DE/CN address format rules documented for V3 when those regions go live.

## 8. Honorifics in visitor-facing copy

| Lang | Visitor address | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| TC | 您 (singular) / 各位 (plural) / 您家人 (family) | "請您稍候" |
| EN | "you" — title + last name optional; default to first name unless customer cued formality | "Please, Mr. Smith — this way" |
| JP | お客様 / 〇〇様 / sonkeigo verbs | "お客様、こちらへどうぞ" |

Staff-facing internal copy:
| Lang | Self-reference | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| TC | 我們 / 同仁 | no honorifics |
| EN | "we" / "the team" | no honorifics |
| JP | 私ども / 同仁 | です/ます is enough; no sonkeigo about staff |

## 9. Writing direction (RTL preparation)

V2 ships LTR only. The trilingual contract is written with logical CSS properties where added:

- New CSS uses `margin-inline-start` / `padding-inline-end` instead of `margin-left` / `padding-right` (per P3 commitment in plan).
- Legacy CSS retains directional properties; not refactored.

Future languages (Arabic, Hebrew): the `.tri-*` pattern will need:
- `dir="rtl"` on the `<html>` when `data-lang="ar"` is added.
- Mirror BARS grid, scenario disclosure indicators, KPI tags.
- This is a V3+ effort gated on actual need.

## 10. Out of scope for V2

- Cultural taboo lists (Module M5 curriculum content — Module C10 has it as v0.1 outline; full content blocked on SME per James #8).
- Gesture maps per region.
- Proxemics charts.
- Currency conversion automation.

These are content concerns; the rules above are infrastructure. Content lives in `/curriculum/m5/` once SME-filled.
