# 4-Dimension Pragmatic Rubric v1

Score each dimension 1-5. These are independent of `urgencyScore`; together they ground urgency in language-level evidence.

## 1. Threat escalation language (`threatEscalation`)

How the article frames the event's trajectory. Not what the event is — how language describes its direction.

- 1 — Descriptive / past-tense / informational ("a protest took place yesterday")
- 2 — Stable concern ("tensions remain")
- 3 — Active development with hedged language ("could escalate", "officials warned")
- 4 — Active escalation language with concrete claims ("forces moving in", "executive order signed effective immediately")
- 5 — Crisis-frame language with imminence ("hours away", "first casualties reported", "fab on lockdown")

## 2. Actor specificity (`actorSpecificity`)

How precisely the article names the actors driving the event.

- 1 — Anonymous / no actor ("rumors", "some people")
- 2 — Vague collective ("protesters", "the regime")
- 3 — Named organization or government body
- 4 — Named organization **and** named individual with role
- 5 — Named individuals with role, count, and verifiable identifiers (statement, press release)

## 3. Geographic specificity (`geoSpecificity`)

How precisely the article ties the event to a place that matters for GPS.

- 1 — Continent or region only ("Asia", "Europe")
- 2 — Country level
- 3 — Sub-national (state, province, prefecture, city)
- 4 — Distinct neighborhood or precise distance from a fab ("8 km from the Kumamoto fab")
- 5 — On or directly adjacent to a TSMC site / supplier site / critical logistics node

## 4. Time urgency (`timeUrgency`)

How time-bounded the action implication is.

- 1 — Historical reference / explainer / no time signal
- 2 — Months out / no deadline implied
- 3 — Weeks out / scheduled event with notice
- 4 — Days / effective within the week
- 5 — Today / unfolding now / next 24h

## Calibration note

A `5/5/5/5` should occur in well under 5% of articles. If you find yourself scoring 5/5/5/5, ask whether the article is really crisis-grade or whether you're being generous. Don't anchor to round numbers — 3/2/4/3 is a normal pattern.
